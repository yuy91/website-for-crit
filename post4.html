Watching films so you don't have to.
1. The Shawshank Redemption
Two guys digging their way out of prison for two hours, with endless talk about “hope.” The movie is supposed to be deep, but honestly, it’s just two guys wasting time in prison. The escape is the only exciting part, but even that feels like a long wait for something that barely makes a dent.
★★☆☆☆
2. La La Land
Two people whining about their “dreams” in LA while dancing around in a movie that’s meant to be inspiring. It’s like a first-world problem the movie. The ending? Tears for no reason.
★★☆☆☆
3. Eternal Sunshine of the Spotless Mind
A movie about erasing memories of an ex, which sounds like a good idea until the movie becomes a confusing mess of strange visuals and random philosophical ideas.
★★★☆☆
4. Her
A guy falls in love with his operating system. It’s weird and awkward, mostly just makes you question the idea of AI relationships.
★★☆☆☆
5. The Social Network
Facebook was created by a guy who can’t even get along with his best friend. By the end, you’ll be too busy thinking about deleting your social media than caring about Zuckerberg’s rise.
★★★☆☆
6. Fight Club
A film about a guy who’s fed up with his life and starts an underground fight club. It feels more like an excuse to punch people than some philosophical revelation.
★★★☆☆
7. Donnie Darko
Time travel, creepy rabbits, and teenage angst—basically a movie that tries too hard to be deep. All confusion with no real payoff.
★★☆☆☆
8. The Graduate
A confused guy dating an older woman and trying to figure his life out. Overhyped as a “classic”.
★★☆☆☆
